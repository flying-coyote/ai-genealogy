# German Church Records: Archion and Matricula

Digitized German and Central European parish registers, primarily Lutheran and Catholic.

## Overview

German parish registers (Kirchenbucher) are the primary source for German genealogy before civil registration. Civil registration began:
- Baden: 1869
- Prussia: 1874
- German Empire (all states): 1876

Before those dates, church registers are the primary record type. Two major online platforms serve most digitized German church records: Archion (Protestant focus) and Matricula (Catholic focus). Coverage is by diocese or church district — check which platform covers your target region before subscribing or searching.

---

## Archion

`https://archion.de/`

**What it is**: Primarily Lutheran and Protestant (Evangelische Kirche) parish registers for Germany and some adjacent areas. Largest digital collection of German Protestant church records.

**Access**: Subscription-based. See `https://archion.de/pricing` for current monthly and annual options.

**Coverage**: 17M+ scanned images. Best coverage for Baden-Wurttemberg, Bavaria (Protestant areas), Hessen, Rhineland-Palatinate, and Wurttemberg. Weaker for historically Catholic Bavaria and former East Germany (those records are often on Matricula instead).

**Browse path**: Collection → Church district (Kirchenkreis) → Parish (Gemeinde) → Register type (Taufen/Heiraten/Begrabnisse) → Year range → Page browse.

**Name search**: Limited name search for indexed collections. Most registers require page-by-page browsing. Know the approximate year and parish before starting a manual browse — otherwise the volume is unmanageable.

**Evidence tier**: Tier 1 (original parish register images).

---

## Matricula

`https://matricula-online.eu/`

**What it is**: Catholic parish registers for Germany, Austria, Czech Republic, Poland, Hungary, and other Central European countries. Administered cooperatively by Austrian Catholic dioceses and German Catholic dioceses.

**Access**: Free. No login required for most collections.

**Coverage**: Strong for: Austria (all dioceses), Bavaria (Catholic), Czech Republic, Silesia, parts of Poland. German Protestant records are not here — use Archion for those.

**Browse path**: `https://matricula-online.eu/` → country → diocese → parish → collection type → year → page.

**Direct URL pattern**:
```
https://data.matricula-online.eu/en/{country}/{diocese}/{parish}/{register-code}/
```

**Evidence tier**: Tier 1 (original parish register images).

## Choosing Between Archion and Matricula

| Confession | Primary Platform | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lutheran / Evangelical | Archion | Covers most of Germany's Protestant regions |
| Catholic (German) | Matricula | Also check diocesan archives directly for gaps |
| Catholic (Austrian) | Matricula | Full Austrian coverage |
| Catholic (Polish-speaking Silesia) | Matricula | Also on Geneteka for indexed version |
| Reformed (Calvinist) | Archion (partial) | Some Reformed registers also on FamilySearch |
| Jewish | Neither | See JRI-Poland, Yad Vashem, Leo Baeck Institute |

## FamilySearch Catalog as Complement

Many German church registers not on Archion or Matricula are on FamilySearch microfilm, unindexed. Always check FS Catalog by location after checking both platforms:

```
https://www.familysearch.org/search/catalog?place=Germany&subject=Church%20records
```

Navigate: Catalog → Germany → state → county → town/parish → film list. Then browse the film manually page-by-page.

## What Kirchenbucher Record

| Register Type | German Term | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| Baptism | Taufen | Child name, baptism date, parents, godparents |
| Marriage | Heiraten / Trauungen | Groom and bride names, parents, witnesses |
| Burial | Begrabnisse / Beerdigungen | Deceased name, burial date, age or birth year |
| Confirmation | Konfirmationen | Child name, parents, confirmation date |
| Communicants lists | Kommunikantenlisten | Annual lists of confirmed members by household |

Communicants lists are particularly useful for placing a family in a specific year and household — they often survive when individual event records do not.

## German Research Context

**Script**: Records are almost always in Kurrent script (pre-1940). Use Transkribus for transcription assistance (see `transkribus.md`). Do not attempt to search or index records without first having a transcription.

**Village name changes**: German village names in present-day Poland and Czech Republic changed after 1945. A village called "Neustadt" in 1890 records may now be "Prudnik" or "Nove Mesto." Use historical gazetteers to find historical names and their modern equivalents:
- Meyers Orts- und Verkehrs-Lexikon des Deutschen Reichs (1912) — scanned at archive.org
- Gemeindeverzeichnis Deutschland — historical administrative gazetteer

**Evangelical church district organization**: German Protestant parishes are organized by Kirchenkreis (church district), not diocese. A village may belong to a different Kirchenkreis than the nearest large town. Check the Archion browse tree carefully — the parish you want may be filed under an unexpected district.

**Austrian records in Matricula**: Austrian parish registers are among the most complete in Europe. Catholic records for Austria go back to the late 1600s in many parishes, and some to the 1500s. Matricula's Austrian coverage is essentially complete for surviving registers.

## Research Sequence for German Ancestors

1. Identify likely religion (Catholic or Protestant) from family tradition, region, or surname patterns.
2. Check Archion (Protestant) or Matricula (Catholic) for the specific village.
3. If not found on either platform: check FamilySearch Catalog by location.
4. If FS Catalog microfilm exists but is unindexed: browse manually by approximate year.
5. If no digital access: contact the relevant diocesan or state archive directly — many have not yet been digitized.
6. For Kurrent transcription: use Transkribus on downloaded images.
